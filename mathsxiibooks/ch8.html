8.1 Introduction to Conic Sections

Conic sections are curves formed by the intersection of a plane and a double-napped cone. The main types are the circle, ellipse, parabola, and hyperbola. The circle is a special case where the plane intersects the cone perpendicular to its axis.

Definition of a Circle

A circle is the set of all points in a plane that are equidistant from a fixed point called the center. The distance from the center to any point on the circle is called the radius.

8.2 The Equation of a Circle

We can describe a circle algebraically with an equation. The two main forms are the standard form and the general form.

Standard Form

The standard form of the equation of a circle with center \((h, k)\) and radius \(r\) is:

\[ (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2 \]

If the center is at the origin \((0, 0)\), the equation simplifies to \(x^2 + y^2 = r^2\).

General Form

The general form of the equation of a circle is:

\[ x^2 + y^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0 \]
  • Center: \((-g, -f)\)
  • Radius: \(r = \sqrt{g^2 + f^2 - c}\)

For this to represent a real circle, we must have \(g^2 + f^2 - c > 0\).

8.3 Tangents and Normals to a Circle

A tangent is a line that touches the circle at exactly one point. A normal is a line perpendicular to the tangent at the point of tangency.

Condition for Tangency

A line \(y = mx + c\) is tangent to the circle \(x^2 + y^2 = r^2\) if the perpendicular distance from the center \((0,0)\) to the line is equal to the radius \(r\). This leads to the condition:

\[ c^2 = r^2(1 + m^2) \]

Equation of a Tangent at a Point

The equation of the tangent to the circle \(x^2 + y^2 = r^2\) at a point \((x_1, y_1)\) on the circle is:

\[ xx_1 + yy_1 = r^2 \]

The normal line at this point always passes through the center of the circle.

Length of a Tangent

The length of the tangent from an external point \((x_1, y_1)\) to the circle \(x^2 + y^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0\) is given by the formula:

\[ L = \sqrt{x_1^2 + y_1^2 + 2gx_1 + 2fy_1 + c} \]

8.4 Geometric Properties of Circles

Circles have several fundamental geometric properties that can be proven using analytic methods.

Key Properties

  • Perpendicular Bisector of a Chord: The perpendicular bisector of any chord of a circle passes through the center of the circle.
  • Angle in a Semicircle: The angle subtended by a diameter at any point on the circumference is always a right angle (90¬∞).
  • Tangents from an External Point: The lengths of the two tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal.
  • Congruent Chords: Chords of equal length are equidistant from the center, and vice versa.
