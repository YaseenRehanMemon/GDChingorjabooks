Faraday's Law of Induction

The core principle of electromagnetic induction was discovered by Michael Faraday. It states that a changing magnetic field through a loop of wire induces an electromotive force (EMF), or voltage, in the wire.

Faraday's Law of Induction

\[ \mathcal{E} = -N \frac{\Delta \Phi_B}{\Delta t} \]

\(\mathcal{E}\) = Induced EMF (in Volts)
\(N\) = Number of turns in the coil
\(\Delta \Phi_B\) = Change in magnetic flux
\(\Delta t\) = Time interval

Lenz's Law: The Direction of the Current

The negative sign in Faraday's Law is crucial and is represented by Lenz's Law. It states that the direction of the induced current is such that its own magnetic field opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it. This is a statement of the conservation of energy; the induced current creates a field that fights the change, meaning you have to do work to create it.

Motional EMF

An EMF can be induced not just by a changing field, but also by moving a conductor through a constant magnetic field. This is called motional EMF.

How it Works

When a conducting rod of length \(L\) moves with velocity \(v\) perpendicular to a magnetic field \(B\), the magnetic force on the charge carriers (\(F = qvB\)) pushes them to one end of the rod. This separation of charge creates an electric field and thus a potential difference, or EMF, across the ends of the rod.

Motional EMF Formula

\[ \mathcal{E} = B L v \]

Generators and Motors

Electromagnetic induction is the fundamental principle behind electric generators and motors.

AC Generator

A generator converts mechanical energy into electrical energy. It works by rotating a coil of wire in a magnetic field. As the coil rotates, the magnetic flux through it changes continuously, inducing a sinusoidal alternating current (AC) according to Faraday's Law.

Electric Motor and Back EMF

A motor converts electrical energy into mechanical energy. It is essentially a generator run in reverse. When a current is passed through a coil in a magnetic field, the coil experiences a torque and rotates. As the motor's coil rotates, it also acts like a generator, producing an induced EMF that opposes the original current. This is called back EMF, and it is crucial for regulating the motor's speed and current draw.

Inductance

Inductance is the property of an electrical conductor by which a change in current through it induces an electromotive force in itself and in any nearby conductors.

Self-Inductance (L)

When the current in a coil changes, the coil induces an EMF in itself. This is called self-inductance. A component designed to have a specific inductance is called an inductor.

\[ \mathcal{E} = -L \frac{\Delta I}{\Delta t} \]

Energy stored in an inductor's magnetic field is \(U = \frac{1}{2}LI^2\).

Mutual Inductance (M)

A changing current in one coil can induce an EMF in a second, nearby coil. This is called mutual inductance.

\[ \mathcal{E}_2 = -M \frac{\Delta I_1}{\Delta t} \]

Transformers

A transformer is a device that uses mutual inductance to change the voltage of an AC signal. It is essential for efficient long-distance power transmission.

How it Works

It consists of two coils, a primary and a secondary, wound on a common iron core. An alternating current in the primary coil creates a changing magnetic flux in the core, which then induces an EMF in the secondary coil.

The Transformer Equation

\[ \frac{V_S}{V_P} = \frac{N_S}{N_P} \]

Step-Up Transformer: \(N_S > N_P\), so voltage increases (and current decreases).

Step-Down Transformer: \(N_S < N_P\), so voltage decreases (and current increases).

Power is transmitted at very high voltages to minimize energy loss (\(P_{loss} = I^2R\)) and then stepped down for safe use in homes.

Chapter Summary

Electromagnetic induction is the link between magnetism and electricity, forming the basis for much of modern technology.

  • Faraday's Law of Induction: A changing magnetic flux induces an EMF (\(\mathcal{E} = -N \Delta\Phi_B / \Delta t\)).
  • Lenz's Law: The induced current creates a magnetic field that opposes the change that produced it, conserving energy.
  • Motional EMF (\(\mathcal{E} = BLv\)) is induced when a conductor moves through a magnetic field.
  • Generators use motional EMF to convert mechanical energy to electrical energy. Motors do the reverse, and their rotation creates a back EMF that regulates their operation.
  • Inductance is the property of a circuit to oppose a change in current. Self-inductance occurs within a single coil, while mutual inductance occurs between two coils.
  • Transformers use mutual inductance to step AC voltages up or down, enabling efficient power transmission.
