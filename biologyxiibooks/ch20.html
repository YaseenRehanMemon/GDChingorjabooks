1. Male Reproductive System

The male reproductive system is designed to produce and deliver sperm for fertilization.

Anatomy

  • Testes (Gonads): Produce sperm through a process called spermatogenesis and secrete the primary male hormone, testosterone.
  • Accessory Ducts: A series of tubes (epididymis, ductus deferens, ejaculatory duct) that store and transport sperm.
  • Accessory Glands: The seminal vesicles, prostate gland, and bulbourethral glands produce fluids that mix with sperm to form semen.

Hormonal Control in Males

The hypothalamus secretes GnRH, which stimulates the anterior pituitary to release FSH and LH.

  • FSH (Follicle-Stimulating Hormone): Promotes sperm production in the testes.
  • LH (Luteinizing Hormone): Stimulates Leydig cells in the testes to produce testosterone.
  • Testosterone: Responsible for the development of male secondary sexual characteristics and maintaining the reproductive tract.

2. Female Reproductive System

The female reproductive system is designed to produce eggs, receive sperm, provide a site for fertilization and fetal development, and give birth.

Anatomy

  • Ovaries (Gonads): Produce eggs (ova) through oogenesis and secrete the primary female hormones, estrogen and progesterone.
  • Oviducts (Fallopian Tubes): The site where fertilization typically occurs. They transport the egg from the ovary to the uterus.
  • Uterus: A muscular organ where the embryo implants and develops during pregnancy. Its inner lining is called the endometrium.
  • Vagina: Receives sperm during intercourse and serves as the birth canal.

3. The Ovarian and Menstrual Cycles

The female reproductive cycle involves two interconnected cycles that are regulated by hormones: the ovarian cycle and the menstrual (uterine) cycle. An average cycle is about 28 days.

The Ovarian Cycle

This cycle describes events in the ovary.

  • Follicular Phase (Days 1-14): FSH stimulates the growth of a follicle in the ovary. The growing follicle secretes estrogen.
  • Ovulation (Day 14): A surge in LH triggers the release of the mature egg from the follicle.
  • Luteal Phase (Days 14-28): The ruptured follicle develops into the corpus luteum, which secretes progesterone and some estrogen.

The Menstrual (Uterine) Cycle

This cycle describes changes in the uterus lining (endometrium).

  • Menstruation (Days 1-5): If pregnancy does not occur, the corpus luteum degenerates, hormone levels fall, and the endometrium is shed.
  • Proliferative Phase (Days 6-14): Rising estrogen from the follicle causes the endometrium to rebuild.
  • Secretory Phase (Days 15-28): Progesterone from the corpus luteum causes the endometrium to thicken further and develop glands, preparing it for implantation.

4. Disorders of the Reproductive System

Various issues can lead to infertility, the inability to conceive after one year of regular, unprotected intercourse.

Causes of Male Infertility

  • Abnormal sperm production (low count, poor motility).
  • Blockages in the reproductive tract.
  • Hormonal imbalances or lifestyle factors.

Causes of Female Infertility

  • Ovulation Disorders: Irregular or absent ovulation (e.g., from PCOS).
  • Fallopian Tube Blockage: Prevents the egg and sperm from meeting.
  • Uterine Issues: Conditions that prevent embryo implantation.

In Vitro Fertilization (IVF) is a common treatment where an egg is fertilized by sperm in a lab, and the resulting embryo is transferred to the uterus.

5. Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STDs)

STDs are infections transmitted through sexual contact.

Common STDs

  • Gonorrhea & Syphilis: Bacterial infections that are treatable with antibiotics.
  • Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS): Caused by the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV), which attacks the immune system. There is no cure, but antiviral treatments can manage the infection.

Chapter Summary

  • Male System: Produces sperm and testosterone, regulated by FSH and LH.
  • Female System: Produces eggs, estrogen, and progesterone. It provides the site for fertilization and fetal development.
  • Reproductive Cycles: The female system is governed by the 28-day ovarian and menstrual cycles, which are controlled by complex hormonal feedback loops involving FSH, LH, estrogen, and progesterone.
  • Fertilization: The fusion of sperm and egg, typically in the oviduct, which forms a zygote and initiates pregnancy.
  • Disorders: Infertility can affect both males and females. STDs are infections spread through sexual contact.
