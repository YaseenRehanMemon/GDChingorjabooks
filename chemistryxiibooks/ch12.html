1. The Atmosphere and Air Pollution

Environmental chemistry studies chemical processes in the environment. The atmosphere is divided into layers, with the troposphere being the lowest layer where we live and where weather occurs.

Major Air Pollutants

The combustion of fossil fuels releases several harmful pollutants:

  • Oxides of Sulfur (\( \text{SO}_x \)): \( \text{SO}_2 \) and \( \text{SO}_3 \) are released from burning coal and are primary causes of industrial smog and acid rain.
  • Oxides of Nitrogen (\( \text{NO}_x \)): \( \text{NO} \) and \( \text{NO}_2 \) are produced at high temperatures in car engines and contribute to smog and acid rain.
  • Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs): Unburnt hydrocarbons from gasoline and solvents that contribute to ozone formation in the troposphere.

2. Acid Rain

Acid rain refers to any form of precipitation with acidic components, such as sulfuric or nitric acid, that fall to the ground from the atmosphere in wet or dry forms.

Formation of Acid Rain

Sulfur oxides and nitrogen oxides react with water, oxygen, and other chemicals in the atmosphere to form sulfuric acid (\( \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4 \)) and nitric acid (\( \text{HNO}_3 \)). This lowers the pH of rain from its natural level (around 5.6) to 4.0 or lower.

Impacts: Acid rain damages forests, acidifies lakes and streams (harming aquatic life), and corrodes buildings and statues.

3. The Greenhouse Effect and Global Warming

What is the Greenhouse Effect?

A natural process where certain gases in the atmosphere, known as greenhouse gases (e.g., \( \text{CO}_2 \), \( \text{CH}_4 \), \( \text{H}_2\text{O} \)), trap heat from the sun. This effect keeps the Earth's surface warm enough to support life.

Global Warming

Human activities, primarily the burning of fossil fuels, have drastically increased the concentration of greenhouse gases, especially \( \text{CO}_2 \). This enhances the natural greenhouse effect, leading to a gradual increase in the Earth's average surface temperature, a phenomenon known as global warming or climate change.

4. Ozone Layer Depletion

The stratosphere contains a layer of ozone (\( \text{O}_3 \)) that protects life on Earth by absorbing most of the sun's harmful ultraviolet (UV) radiation.

The Role of CFCs

Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), once widely used in refrigeration and aerosol sprays, are the primary cause of ozone depletion. In the stratosphere, UV radiation breaks down CFCs, releasing chlorine atoms. A single chlorine atom can act as a catalyst to destroy tens of thousands of ozone molecules.

The Catalytic Cycle

\( \text{Cl} \cdot + \text{O}_3 \rightarrow \text{ClO} \cdot + \text{O}_2 \)

\( \text{ClO} \cdot + \text{O} \rightarrow \text{Cl} \cdot + \text{O}_2 \)

The chlorine radical is regenerated, allowing it to destroy more ozone molecules.

International agreements like the Montreal Protocol have successfully phased out the production of CFCs, and the ozone layer is slowly recovering.

5. Water Pollution and Analysis

Water pollution is any change in the quality of water that adversely affects life. It is caused by industrial waste, sewage, and agricultural runoff.

Parameters for Drinking Water Quality

The World Health Organization (WHO) sets standards for safe drinking water, including:

  • pH: Should be between 6.5 and 8.5.
  • Total Dissolved Solids (TDS): A measure of all inorganic and organic suspended particles. Should be below 600 mg/L.
  • Turbidity: A measure of the cloudiness of water.
  • Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD): A measure of the amount of oxygen consumed by bacteria while decomposing organic waste. High BOD indicates pollution.

Chapter Summary

  • Air Pollution: Caused by \( \text{SO}_x \), \( \text{NO}_x \), and VOCs from burning fossil fuels.
  • Acid Rain: Formed when \( \text{SO}_x \) and \( \text{NO}_x \) react with atmospheric water. It damages ecosystems and structures.
  • Greenhouse Effect: A natural process where gases like \( \text{CO}_2 \) trap heat. Enhanced by human activity, it leads to global warming.
  • Ozone Layer: Protects Earth from UV radiation. It has been depleted by CFCs, which catalytically destroy ozone molecules in the stratosphere.
  • Water Pollution: The contamination of water bodies. Water quality is assessed using parameters like pH, TDS, and BOD.
