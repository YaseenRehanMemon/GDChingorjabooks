1. Introduction to Industrial Chemistry

Industrial chemistry is the branch of chemistry that applies chemical and physical processes to transform raw materials into products that are beneficial to humanity. It plays a crucial role in a country's economy by producing essential goods like medicines, plastics, paints, and fertilizers.

2. Pharmaceutical Industry

The pharmaceutical industry discovers, develops, produces, and markets drugs or pharmaceutical drugs for use as medications to be administered to patients to cure them, vaccinate them, or alleviate symptoms.

Common Types of Drugs

  • Analgesics: Pain relievers (e.g., Aspirin, Paracetamol).
  • Antibiotics: Kill or inhibit the growth of bacteria (e.g., Penicillin).
  • Antipyretics: Reduce fever (e.g., Paracetamol).
  • Antihistamines: Treat allergies by reducing histamine levels (e.g., Diphenhydramine).

3. Synthetic Polymers

Polymers are large molecules (macromolecules) made up of many repeating smaller units called monomers. Synthetic polymers are created through chemical reactions and are essential materials in modern life.

Key Synthetic Polymers

  • Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC): An addition polymer made from vinyl chloride monomers. It is a versatile thermoplastic used for pipes, bottles, and electrical insulation due to its durability and low cost.
  • Nylon 6,6: A polyamide formed by the condensation polymerization of hexamethylenediamine and adipic acid. It is known for its high strength and is used to make textiles, ropes, and bristles.

4. Cosmetics

Cosmetics are products used to enhance or alter the appearance of the face, body, nails, or hair.

Composition of Common Cosmetics

  • Lipstick: A mixture of oils, waxes, pigments, and moisturizers.
  • Nail Polish: A lacquer containing pigments, a film-forming polymer (like nitrocellulose), plasticizers, and a solvent.
  • Nail Polish Remover: An organic solvent, typically acetone, designed to dissolve the polymers in nail polish.

5. Adhesives

Adhesives, or glues, are substances that bond materials together. They create a bond through physical or chemical processes.

Types of Adhesives

  • Starch: A natural, low-strength adhesive made by heating starch in water. Used in food products and for biodegradable plastics.
  • Epoxy Resins: Strong synthetic adhesives used for bonding metals, plastics, and ceramics.
  • Super Glue (Cyanoacrylate): A fast-acting adhesive that bonds quickly to many surfaces.
  • Wood Glue (Polyvinyl Acetate): A water-based adhesive that forms a strong, durable bond between pieces of wood.

Chapter Summary

  • Industrial Chemistry: Focuses on converting raw materials into useful products for society.
  • Pharmaceuticals: Includes the production of essential medicines like analgesics (painkillers) and antibiotics (for bacterial infections).
  • Synthetic Polymers: Large molecules like PVC (used in pipes) and Nylon (used in fabrics) are created through polymerization and are vital to modern industry.
  • Cosmetics: Products like lipstick and nail polish are formulated mixtures of chemicals designed to enhance appearance.
  • Adhesives: Substances like epoxy resins and super glue are used to bond materials together in construction and everyday life.
