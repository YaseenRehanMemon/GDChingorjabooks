4.1 Introduction to Sequences

A sequence (or progression) is an ordered set of numbers formed according to a definite rule. Each number in the sequence is called a term or element.

4.1.1 General Term (\(a_n\))

A sequence is often defined by its general term \(a_n\), which is a formula that expresses any term in relation to its position \(n\) in the sequence.

Example: For \(a_n = 5n+1\), the terms are \(6, 11, 16, 21, \dots\).

4.1.2 Special Sequences

  • Triangular Sequence: Numbers of dots that form equilateral triangles (\(1, 3, 6, 10, \dots\)). The \(n\)-th term is \(T_n = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}\).
  • Factorial Sequence: Involves factorials (\(n!\)), where \(n! = n \times (n-1) \times \dots \times 1\). For example, \(\frac{1}{n!}\).
  • Pascal Sequence: The coefficients in Pascal's Triangle, related to binomial expansions.

4.2 Arithmetic Sequences & Series

4.2.1 Arithmetic Sequence (A.P.)

An arithmetic sequence (or arithmetic progression) is a sequence where each term after the first is obtained by adding a fixed number, called the common difference (\(d\)), to the preceding term.

General Term (\(a_n\)) of an A.P.

\[ a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d \]

Where \(a_1\) is the first term and \(d\) is the common difference.

4.2.2 Arithmetic Series

An arithmetic series is the sum of the terms of an arithmetic sequence.

Sum of \(n\) terms (\(S_n\)) of an A.P.

\[ S_n = \frac{n}{2}(2a_1 + (n-1)d) \]

or, if \(a_l\) is the last term:

\[ S_n = \frac{n}{2}(a_1 + a_l) \]

4.2.3 Arithmetic Mean (A.M.)

The arithmetic mean between two numbers \(a\) and \(b\) is \(\frac{a+b}{2}\). We can insert \(n\) arithmetic means between \(a\) and \(b\) to form an A.P.

4.3 Geometric Sequences & Series

4.3.1 Geometric Sequence (G.P.)

A geometric sequence (or geometric progression) is a sequence where each term after the first is obtained by multiplying the preceding term by a fixed non-zero number, called the common ratio (\(r\)).

General Term (\(a_n\)) of a G.P.

\[ a_n = a_1 r^{n-1} \]

Where \(a_1\) is the first term and \(r\) is the common ratio.

4.3.2 Geometric Series

A geometric series is the sum of the terms of a geometric sequence.

Sum of \(n\) terms (\(S_n\)) of a G.P.

\[ S_n = \frac{a_1(1-r^n)}{1-r} \quad \text{if } r \neq 1 \]

If \(r=1\), \(S_n = na_1\).

4.3.3 Infinite Geometric Series

An infinite geometric series converges (has a finite sum) if \(|r| < 1\).

Sum to Infinity (\(S_\infty\)) of a G.P.

\[ S_\infty = \frac{a_1}{1-r} \quad \text{if } |r| < 1 \]

4.3.4 Geometric Mean (G.M.)

The geometric mean between two positive numbers \(a\) and \(b\) is \(\pm\sqrt{ab}\).

4.4 Harmonic Sequences & Means

4.4.1 Harmonic Sequence (H.P.)

A harmonic sequence (or harmonic progression) is a sequence where the reciprocals of its terms form an arithmetic sequence.

General Term (\(T_n\)) of an H.P.

If \(\frac{1}{a_1}, \frac{1}{a_2}, \frac{1}{a_3}, \dots\) is an A.P. with first term \(A\) and common difference \(D\), then the terms of the H.P. are \(T_n = \frac{1}{A + (n-1)D}\).

4.4.2 Harmonic Mean (H.M.)

The harmonic mean between two numbers \(a\) and \(b\) is \(\frac{2ab}{a+b}\).

4.4.3 Relation between A.M., G.M., and H.M.

For any two positive numbers \(a\) and \(b\), the arithmetic mean (A.M.), geometric mean (G.M.), and harmonic mean (H.M.) are related:

  • \(A \ge G \ge H\).
  • \(G^2 = AH\).

Chapter Summary

  • Sequence: Ordered list of numbers following a rule.
  • Arithmetic Progression (A.P.): Constant common difference \((d)\). \(a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d\), \(S_n = \frac{n}{2}(a_1 + a_l)\).
  • Geometric Progression (G.P.): Constant common ratio \((r)\). \(a_n = a_1 r^{n-1}\), \(S_n = \frac{a_1(1-r^n)}{1-r}\). Infinite G.P. sums to \(\frac{a_1}{1-r}\) if \(|r|<1\).
  • Harmonic Progression (H.P.): Reciprocals form an A.P.
  • Means: Arithmetic Mean \((A = \frac{a+b}{2})\), Geometric Mean \((G = \sqrt{ab})\), Harmonic Mean \((H = \frac{2ab}{a+b})\).
  • Relationship: \(A \ge G \ge H\) and \(G^2 = AH\).
